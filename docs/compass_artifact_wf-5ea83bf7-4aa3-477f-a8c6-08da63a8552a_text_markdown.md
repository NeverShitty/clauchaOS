# Comprehensive Your County Illinois Domestic Relations Division Pleading Requirements and Formatting Standards

## FORMATTING REQUIREMENTS

### Precise Caption Formatting
**Required Caption Elements:**
- **Header**: "IN THE CIRCUIT COURT OF Your County, ILLINOIS"
- **Division**: "DOMESTIC RELATIONS DIVISION" 
- **District**: If filed in suburban district, include district number (e.g., "SECOND MUNICIPAL DISTRICT - SKOKIE")
- **Case Title**: 
  - Initial pleadings: "IN RE THE MARRIAGE OF" followed by parties' names
  - Plaintiff's name must be placed first (IL Supreme Court Rule 131(b))
  - Each party name on separate line beginning at left margin
- **Case Number**: Four-digit year + division letter "D" + sequential numbers
  - Example: 2025D123456
  - Suburban districts include district number: 2025D400012
- **Judge Assignment**: "Calendar [X]" or "Honorable [Judge Name]"

### Page Margins, Font Requirements, Line Spacing
**Mandatory Specifications per Illinois Court Rules:**
- **Margins**: Minimum 1 inch on all sides
- **Font**: 
  - Body text: Minimum 12-point font
  - Footnotes: Minimum 10-point font
  - Recommended fonts: Cambria, Georgia, or Helvetica
- **Line Spacing**: 1.5 or double-spaced recommended (not specified in rules)
- **Page Size**: 8½ x 11 inches, white paper, black text
- **First Page**: Top right 2" x 2" corner must be left blank for Clerk's stamp
- **Page Numbering**: Consecutive numbering at bottom of page with brief document description

### eFileIL Submission Procedures for Pro Se Attorneys
**Mandatory Requirements:**
- **Attorney Code**: Must use code **99500** in "Your County Attorney Code" field
- **System**: Odyssey eFileIL or other certified Electronic Filing Service Provider (EFSP)
- **File Format**: PDF only, preferably text-searchable
- **Filing Hours**: 
  - Monday-Friday 8:30 AM to 11:59 PM: Filed same day
  - After hours/weekends: Deemed filed next business day at 8:30 AM
- **Electronic Signature**: /s/ followed by printed name

### Emergency Motion Submission Requirements
**Emergency Motion Procedures:**
- **Definition**: "Sudden and unforeseen circumstance that may cause injury, loss of life, or damage to property requiring urgent response" (AO 2021 AO 3(2))
- **Hearing Times**: Most courtrooms 9:00 AM or 9:30 AM Monday-Friday
- **Notice**: 2 days minimum unless court allows shorter notice
- **12:00 PM Coordinator Email**: Not found in current documentation - contact DomesticRelDivservices@cookcountycourt.com for verification
- **Financial Matters**: Generally NOT considered emergencies per Your County policy

## EXHIBIT REQUIREMENTS (Forensic Audit Standards)

### Bates Stamping Requirements
**Format Standards:**
- **Convention**: Party name + sequential number (e.g., "BIANCHI000001")
- **Placement**: Bottom right corner to avoid obscuring content
- **Requirements**: 
  - Digital stamping preferred over manual
  - Apply before final PDF conversion
  - Maintain index/spreadsheet of Bates ranges
  - Unique identifier for each page

### Electronic Filing Requirements
**PDF Standards (GAO 2014-02):**
- **Format**: Mandatory PDF format, text-searchable preferred
- **Bookmarks**: No specific requirement found but recommended for lengthy documents
- **File Size**: No specific Your County limit (typical range 7MB-35MB per document)
- **Multiple Documents**: Must be separate PDF files when filed in same transaction
- **Exhibit Index**: Required for electronically filed exhibits

### Authentication Requirements (Illinois Rule of Evidence 803(6))
**Business Records Exception Requirements:**
1. Made at or near time of event by person with knowledge
2. Kept in course of regularly conducted business activity
3. Regular practice of business to make such records
4. Shown by testimony of custodian OR certification

**LLC Operating Agreements:**
- Custodian certification from authorized member/manager
- Self-authentication if notarized (Rule 902(8))

**Financial Records:**
- Bank records: Certification from bank custodian
- Internal records: Company custodian certification

**Email Communications:**
- Rule 901(b)(4): Distinctive characteristics (headers, metadata)
- Rule 901(b)(9): Evidence about electronic processes
- Hash values for digital integrity verification


### Exhibit Labeling Conventions
**Your County Local Rule 13 Standards:**
- **Plaintiff Exhibits**: Numbered (1, 2, 3, etc.)
- **Defendant Exhibits**: Lettered (A, B, C, etc.)
- **Requirements**:
  - Consistent designation throughout case
  - Prepare exhibit list with brief description
  - Two copies to court and court reporter
  - Each exhibit as separate PDF file

## SERVICE AND PROCEDURAL REQUIREMENTS

### Service Certificate Formatting
**Illinois Supreme Court Rule 11 Requirements:**
- List all parties served by name and address
- Specify method of service (email, mail, personal)
- For represented parties, serve attorney only
- Exception: Limited Scope Appearances - serve both party and attorney
- Your County Rule 2.1(c)(i): Separate document titled "notice of service" or "notice of motion"

### Emergency Hearing Procedures
**General Requirements:**
- Present to assigned judge first
- If unavailable, transfer to emergency judge
- Notice: 2 days minimum (may be shortened by court)
- Ex parte orders: Set status/hearing within 10 days
- Immediate service of ex parte order with underlying petition

### Pro Se Attorney Enhanced Standards
**Special Obligations:**
- Held to same professional standards as represented parties
- Rule 137 sanctions apply for frivolous claims
- Must use attorney code 99500 in all filings
- Subject to all local rules and discovery obligations
- Cannot claim ignorance of procedural rules

## SPECIFIC PLEADING TYPE REQUIREMENTS

### 1. Rule 137 Sanctions Motion (735 ILCS 5/2-611)
**Required Elements:**
- Identify each sanctionable pleading/motion by opposing party
- Specify allegedly sanctionable statements
- Document fees incurred as result
- Numbered paragraphs with separate allegations
- Supporting documentation: Verified complaint or affidavits

### 2. Motion to Vacate Temporary Restraining Order
**Statutory Grounds (735 ILCS 5/11-101):**
- TRO limited to 10 days unless extended "for good cause shown"
- 205-day duration violates statutory limit
- Requirements:
  - 2 days' notice (or shorter as court prescribes)
  - Challenge lack of "immediate and irreparable injury"
  - Request expedited hearing per statute

### 3. Response to Petition for Rule to Show Cause
**Format (Your County Rule 13.8):**
- Explain why no violation occurred
- If violation occurred, explain why not willful
- Address false statements in petition
- Include current compliance efforts
- Jurisdictional challenges if applicable
- Separate numbered affirmative defenses

### 4. Emergency Motion (Business Harm)
**Four-Part Test (Houseknecht v. Zagel):**
1. Clearly ascertained right needing protection
2. Imminent risk of irreparable injury
3. Inadequate remedy at law
4. Likelihood of success on merits

**Documentation Requirements:**
- Verified complaint with specific daily damage facts
- Affidavits showing ongoing financial harm
- Evidence harm is "continuing nature"
- Overcome presumption money damages are adequate
