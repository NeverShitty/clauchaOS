# Your County Illinois Domestic Relations Division Pleading Requirements

## Universal Filing Requirements for All DRD Pleadings

### eFileIL Submission Requirements for Pro Se Attorneys
**Critical Code Requirement**: Pro se attorneys MUST enter code **99500** in the "Your County Attorney Code" field during filing. Failure to include this code results in automatic rejection.

**Document Standards**:
- Format: PDF with Optical Character Recognition (OCR) applied
- File size: Maximum 25MB per document, 50MB per complete filing envelope
- Page size: 8.5" x 11" only
- Scanning: 300-600 dpi, black and white preferred
- Create PDFs directly from word processor when possible

**Registration and Access**:
- Register with certified eFileIL provider (e.g., Odyssey eFileIL) - no registration fees
- Fee waivers available via Illinois Supreme Court Rule 298
- Exemptions available for lack of computer access, ADA qualifying disabilities, or language barriers

### Universal Caption and Formatting Standards

**Mandatory Caption Elements**:
- "IN THE CIRCUIT COURT OF Your County, ILLINOIS"
- "COUNTY DEPARTMENT, DOMESTIC RELATIONS DIVISION"
- Plaintiff's name must be placed first
- Case must be "entitled in the court and cause"

**Formatting Requirements**:
- Font: Minimum 12-point (10-point for footnotes); avoid Times New Roman
- Margins: 1-inch all sides, except 2-inch top margin on first page for clerk's stamp
- Line spacing: 1.5 recommended
- Pages must be consecutively numbered with Arabic numerals

**Signature Requirements for Pro Se Filers**:
- Must personally sign all documents
- Include mailing address, telephone number, and single email address for service
- Electronic signature format: "/s/" followed by printed name
- Signing certifies document is well-grounded in fact and warranted by law

### Exhibit Requirements

**Labeling and Organization**:
- Sequential labeling: "Exhibit A," "Exhibit B," etc.
- Each exhibit must be separate PDF file in eFileIL
- Clear, descriptive filenames (e.g., "Exhibit A - Marriage Certificate.pdf")
- OCR required for all exhibits

**Bates Stamping** (when used):
- Not universally required but recommended for complex cases
- Sequential numbering (e.g., 000001, 000002)
- Place in lower right corner
- Continue numbering across all exhibits

**Electronic Bookmarks**:
- Recommended for lengthy documents
- Create using Adobe Acrobat for navigation
- Not mandatory but enhances usability

### Service Requirements

**Certificate of Service Must Include**:
- Title and case number
- Parties served
- Method of service
- Date of service
- Signature

**Approved Service Methods**:
- Electronic service through eFileIL (automatic for e-filed parties)
- Email to designated addresses
- Personal service
- Certified mail with return receipt
- Licensed process servers (special licensing required as of 2025)
- Your County Sheriff ($5 fee)

## 1. Rule 137 Sanctions Motion (735 ILCS 5/2-611)

### Specific Formatting Requirements

**Caption Requirements**:
- Must include "MOTION FOR SANCTIONS PURSUANT TO ILLINOIS SUPREME COURT RULE 137"
- Cite statutory authority: 735 ILCS 5/2-611

**Required Motion Sections**:
1. Introduction stating relief sought under Rule 137
2. Statement of Facts demonstrating sanctionable conduct
3. Legal Standard section citing Rule 137 requirements
4. Argument showing violation of Rule 137
5. Request for specific sanctions
6. Prayer for relief

### Exhibit Requirements

**Mandatory Attachments**:
- Copy of the offending pleading(s)
- All correspondence related to safe harbor provisions
- Supporting documentation for factual allegations
- Affidavit from attorney detailing reasonable inquiry efforts

**Authentication Requirements**:
- Each exhibit must be properly authenticated
- Affidavits must be based on personal knowledge
- Foundation required for any documentary evidence

### Service Requirements

**Safe Harbor Provisions**:
- Must serve motion on opposing party 21 days before filing with court
- This allows opposing party opportunity to withdraw offending pleading
- Include notice of intent to file if not withdrawn

**Timing Deadlines**:
- 21-day safe harbor period is mandatory
- Motion must be filed within reasonable time after violation discovered
- Response time: Standard 28-day response period applies

**Special Service Requirements**:
- Must serve both attorney and party if limited scope representation
- Service must include all exhibits and supporting documentation
- Proof of safe harbor notice must be included with filing

## 2. Motion to Vacate Temporary Restraining Order

### Your County Specific Procedures

**Motion Format Requirements**:
- Title: "EMERGENCY MOTION TO VACATE TEMPORARY RESTRAINING ORDER"
- Must be verified or supported by affidavit
- Include proposed order language

**Required Allegations**:
- Specific facts showing why TRO should be vacated
- Changed circumstances since TRO entered
- Legal grounds under 735 ILCS 5/11-108
- Why original TRO standards not met

**Burden of Proof**:
- Court decides "upon the weight of the evidence"
- Moving party must show TRO is no longer justified
- Affidavits customary to support factual allegations

### Emergency Filing Requirements

**Emergency Designation**:
- Emergency motions heard without prior notice per Your County Circuit Court Rule 2.2(a)
- Heard on "first come first served" basis
- Must first attempt presentation to assigned judge

**Judge Coordinator Procedures**:
- Contact assigned judge's coordinator first
- If unavailable, contact emergency judge coordinator
- Presiding Judge Hon. Regina Scannicchio: (312) 603-3025
- Note: Specific 12:00 PM deadline referenced may be in judge-specific standing orders

**Expedited Hearing Requirements**:
- Notice by fax/telephone by 4:00 PM day before presentation
- If ex parte, must serve written notice within 48 hours
- Emergency motions given precedence over other matters

### Required Supporting Documentation

**Mandatory Exhibits**:
- Copy of original TRO
- Evidence of changed circumstances
- Supporting affidavits with specific facts
- Any relevant orders or pleadings

**Affidavit Requirements**:
- Based on personal knowledge
- Specific facts, not conclusions
- Address each element of original TRO
- Detail any changed circumstances

## 3. Response to Petition for Rule to Show Cause (Contempt)

### Defensive Pleading Standards

**Response Format Requirements**:
- Title: "RESPONSE TO PETITION FOR RULE TO SHOW CAUSE"
- Must respond to each numbered allegation
- Follow admission/denial format of Illinois Supreme Court Rule 136

**Required Elements**:
1. Paragraph-by-paragraph response to petition allegations
2. Affirmative defenses section
3. Jurisdictional challenges (if applicable)
4. Factual basis for inability to comply defense
5. Request for dismissal or alternative relief

### Jurisdictional Challenges

**Personal Jurisdiction Objections**:
- Must be raised in first responsive pleading or waived
- File special appearance if challenging personal jurisdiction
- Include motion to quash service if applicable

**Subject Matter Jurisdiction**:
- Challenge court's power to hear contempt matter
- Argue if underlying order was void
- Raise if court exceeded statutory authority

**Timing Requirements**:
- Jurisdictional challenges must be raised immediately
- Failure to object constitutes waiver
- Exception: Subject matter jurisdiction can be raised anytime

### Substantive Response Requirements

**Responding to Contempt Allegations**:
- Admit, deny, or state insufficient knowledge for each allegation
- Denials must be specific and in good faith
- Include detailed explanation for any non-compliance

**Affirmative Defenses**:
- Inability to comply (most common)
- Substantial compliance
- Order was unclear or ambiguous
- Changed circumstances
- Equitable defenses

**Affidavit Requirements**:
- Required when asserting inability to comply
- Must detail efforts to comply
- Include financial documentation if monetary contempt
- Personal knowledge required

### Timing and Service

**Response Deadline**:
- 28 days from service of petition (unless court orders otherwise)
- No extensions without court order
- Emergency responses may be filed sooner

**Service Requirements**:
- Serve all parties who have appeared
- Include certificate of service
- Electronic service through eFileIL if parties e-filed

## 4. Emergency Motion for Ongoing Business Harm Relief

### Emergency Standards for Business Harm

**What Constitutes Emergency** (per Administrative Order 2021 AO 3(2)):
- "Sudden and unforeseen circumstance" causing potential injury, loss, or damage
- Requires urgent response and remedial action
- Examples: threatened asset dissipation, business sabotage, customer list destruction

**Required Showing (Four-Factor Test)**:
1. Clearly ascertainable right needing protection
2. Likelihood of success on merits
3. Irreparable harm without relief (most important)
4. No adequate remedy at law

**Business-Specific Harm Standards**:
- Threat to ongoing operations
- Customer goodwill damage
- Competitive harm
- Asset dissipation risk

### Specific Formatting Requirements

**Motion Title and Structure**:
- "EMERGENCY MOTION FOR [SPECIFIC RELIEF] DUE TO ONGOING BUSINESS HARM"
- Verified motion or verified complaint required
- Include statement of emergency nature in opening paragraph

**Required Sections**:
1. Emergency Nature and Factual Basis
2. Specific Business Harm Alleged
3. Relief Sought (TRO, preliminary injunction, asset freeze)
4. Supporting Legal Authority
5. Proposed Order

### Supporting Documentation

**Business Records Required**:
- Current financial statements
- Bank statements showing recent activity
- Corporate documents (articles, bylaws, operating agreements)
- Evidence of spouse's access/control
- Documentation of threatened harmful conduct

**Financial Documentation**:
- Evidence of lost revenue/customers
- Unauthorized transaction records
- Suspicious transfer documentation
- Operational disruption evidence
- Projected continuing harm

**Authentication Standards**:
- Meet Illinois Evidence Rule 803(6) for business records
- Foundation for computer-generated records required
- Describe record-keeping processes
- Industry-standard practices testimony helpful

### Emergency Procedures

**12:00 PM Coordinator Deadline**:
- Contact assigned judge's coordinator by noon for same-day hearing
- Verify current deadline with Presiding Judge's office: (312) 603-3025
- Individual judges may have different requirements

**Notice Requirements**:
- May proceed ex parte if notice would defeat purpose
- Standard: Notice by 4:00 PM day before if possible
- If ex parte, serve written notice within 48 hours

**Available Relief**:
- Asset freezing orders
- Exclusive business control
- Receiver appointment (750 ILCS 5/503(l))
- Operational funding orders
- Trade secret protection
