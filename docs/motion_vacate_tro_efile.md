## III. LEGAL ARGUMENT

### A. Petitioner Lacks Standing to Challenge YOUR_LLC Operations

19. **Illinois LLC law requires membership interest** to assert rights regarding LLC business operations. 805 ILCS 180/1-1 et seq.

20. **Petitioner's unanimous removal on October 14, 2024** terminated any membership rights in YOUR_LLC, eliminating standing to seek injunctive relief regarding business operations.

21. **The TRO petition was filed after Petitioner's removal**, when she possessed no legal interest in the business assets subject to the restraining order.

### B. No Marital Property Rights Exist in YOUR_LLC Assets

22. **Illinois law classifies property acquired with separate funds as non-marital property**, regardless of acquisition during marriage. 750 ILCS 5/503(a)(1).

23. **Comprehensive funding documentation** establishes that 100% of YOUR_LLC assets were purchased with:
    - Pre-marital savings and checking accounts
    - IRA distributions from pre-marital employment
    - Employment settlement proceeds constituting separate property
    - No commingling with marital funds

24. **Petitioner's signed acknowledgments** of Respondent's sole funding and separate property sources constitute admissions precluding marital property claims. *See* Exhibits C-1, C-4.

### C. TRO Obtained Through Material Misrepresentations

25. **Illinois Supreme Court Rule 137** requires good faith basis for factual allegations and prohibits frivolous claims designed to harass or maliciously injure.

26. **Petitioner's TRO petition contained material misrepresentations**, including:
    - Claims of current LLC membership after October 14 removal
    - Assertions of emergency requiring immediate relief despite 16-day delay
    - Misrepresentations regarding occupancy of Morada Mami property
    - False claims regarding exclusion from business information

27. **Documentary evidence contradicts each material assertion** in the TRO petition, establishing bad faith filing to circumvent legitimate business obligations.

### D. Ongoing Irreparable Harm to Separate Property Business

28. **The TRO freezes legitimate business operations** of a properly constituted separate property entity, causing ongoing financial harm without protecting any marital interest.

29. **Business interruption damages** include lost rental income, inability to maintain properties, and disruption of ongoing business relationships. *See* Exhibit E-4.

30. **Continued enforcement serves no legitimate purpose** while imposing substantial costs on separate property business operations.
